\nIt\'s a one punch knockout by Sound Panels but why does Acoustic Foam fall to the floor so easy? The absorption power of Sound absorption Panels compared to Foam is like an elephant compared to a mouse! Sound absorbing Panels use much more dense sound absorption materials then foam.
\n\xc2\xa0
\nProperly built Sound Panels use high density acoustic insulation such as Roxul, Owens Corning, or Johns Manville insulation. Foam is cheap but you\'d be slipping on ice and falling hard if you think foam improves the sound within rooms. By covering walls up with foam you\'ve absorbed the crisp high end and left the rest of the sound in a pile of mud.\xc2\xa0
\n
\nWhat does a muddy room sound like? It\'s when the high end frequency of sound is absorbed and the rest is left untreated, causing the balance of the sound within the room to be thrown way off! Don\'t kill the high end man! Also the material plus design equal effective sound treatment within Sound Panels.
\n\xc2\xa0
\nYou could just cover the entire walls in Acoustic Insulation but then you\'ll absorb too much sound. So now what\'s the problem? You want to maintain some liveness within your recording studio but not absorb too much and end up feeling like your in outer space because man would that be uncomfortable!\xc2\xa0
\n
\nSo the next consideration with Sound Panels is their placement. Please, I\'m begging you don\'t cover the entire surface of the walls, Please Please PLEASE! Just place your Acoustic Panels in critical areas of sound buildup.\xc2\xa0
\n
\nWhat are the critical areas of Sound Buildup? You have corners, first reflection points, the cloud above your head, side reflection points, where the walls meet the ceiling, where the floor meets the front wall, directly in front of your computer monitor, and other locations depending on the purpose of your home recording studio or dedicated recording studio. Sound Panels require proper placement but you see, they don\'t need to be everywhere for crypes sakes! Also consider that not just any Acoustic Panel will do.\xc2\xa0
\n
\nWhat\'s so important about the construction of Sound Panels? Built wrong and you might as well dive into the freezing cold pool in the middle of winter! Sound Panels need high density insulation such as Roxul, open backed design for increased sound absorption, sound absorbing or acoustically transparent fabric, and they need to be built to last and mount properly.
