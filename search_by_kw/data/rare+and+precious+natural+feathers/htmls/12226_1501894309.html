About the Spotted Featherback (Chitala chitala)

\r\n

Spotted featherbacks are also referred to as knifefish or clown knifefish due to their knife-like shaped bodies. Featherbacks are often found in lakes, swamps, and river system backwaters. They prefer slow moving water and can survive at very low oxygen levels. Featherbacks are nocturnal, usually moving and feeding during the twilight hours. They do hunt live prey and will attempt to eat any fish that will fit into their mouth. They are also able to \'breathe\' in air to survive in stagnant waters with low oxygen. Featherbacks are a very distinct silvery grey/tan in coloration with a long, knife shaped body and a very long anal fin that extends most of their ventral side.
